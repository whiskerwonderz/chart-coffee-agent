# Colombia Coffee Guide

Colombia is the world's third-largest coffee producer and renowned for producing consistently high-quality, well-balanced Arabica coffee.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Ranking** | 3rd largest producer globally |
| **Production** | ~12-14 million bags annually |
| **Specialty %** | ~30% graded as specialty |
| **Main Species** | 100% Arabica |
| **Varieties** | Castillo, Colombia, Caturra, Typica |
| **Harvest** | Main: Sept-Dec, Mitaca: April-June |
| **Altitude** | 1,200 - 2,000+ masl |

## Why Colombia is Special

### Geographic Advantages
- Three Andean mountain ranges create diverse microclimates
- Year-round harvest (different regions peak at different times)
- Ideal growing conditions: altitude, rainfall, temperature
- Rich volcanic soils

### Quality Infrastructure
- Strong coffee federation (FNC) support
- Established quality standards
- Extensive washing station network
- Direct trade relationships common

## Major Growing Regions

### Huila

**Colombia's top specialty region.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,500-2,000 masl |
| Processing | Washed, Honey, Natural |
| Harvest | Sept-Dec (main), April-June (mitaca) |

- Bright, complex acidity

- Pitalito

### Nariño

**High-altitude, prized for acidity.**

| Attribute | Details |

- Intense, bright acidity
- Citrus (orange, tangerine)

## Colombian Varieties

### Castillo
- **Background**: Developed by Cenicafé for rust resistance
- **Cup Profile**: Clean, mild, balanced
- **Prevalence**: ~45% of production
- **Notes**: Often unfairly dismissed; quality lots excellent

### Colombia Variety
- **Background**: Earlier rust-resistant hybrid
- **Cup Profile**: Balanced, good acidity
- **Prevalence**: ~20% of production

### Caturra
- **Background**: Bourbon mutation from Brazil
- **Cup Profile**: Bright acidity, complex
- **Prevalence**: ~20% of production
- **Notes**: Higher quality potential, disease susceptible

### Typica
- **Background**: Original variety
- **Cup Profile**: Sweet, clean, delicate
- **Prevalence**: <5% of production
- **Notes**: Rare, sought after for cup quality

### Pink Bourbon
- **Background**: Unique Colombian mutation
- **Cup Profile**: Floral, tea-like, complex
- **Notes**: Premium pricing, limited availability

### Gesha
- **Background**: Grown in select high-altitude farms
- **Cup Profile**: Jasmine, bergamot, tropical
- **Notes**: Competition-quality lots

## Processing in Colombia

### Washed (Fully Washed)
- Dominates Colombian production (~95%)
- Clean, bright cups
- Highlights terroir
- Fermentation: 12-36 hours typical

### Honey Process
- Growing in specialty sector
- Yellow, red, and black honey
- Adds sweetness, body
- Popular in Huila

### Natural Process
- Less common but increasing
- Fruit-forward, heavier body
- Requires careful management
- Found in experimental lots

### Experimental
- Anaerobic fermentation gaining popularity
- Extended fermentation lots
- Thermal shock processing
- Yeast inoculation experiments

## Colombian Grading System

### Export Grades

| Grade | Screen Size | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Supremo | 17+ | Largest, premium |
| Excelso | 14-16 | Standard export |
| UGQ | Mixed | Usual Good Quality |
| Maragogype | 18+ | Large "elephant" bean |

### Quality Designations
- **EP (European Preparation)**: Max 8 defects/300g
- **Specialty Grade**: 80+ cupping score
- **Micro-lot**: Single farm or plot

## Buying Colombian Coffee

### What to Look For
- Region and municipality specified
- Farm name (for micro-lots)
- Variety information
- Processing method
- Altitude
- Harvest date

### Seasonality
- Main crop fresh arrivals: January-April
- Mitaca fresh arrivals: July-September
- Year-round availability from different regions

### Price Expectations
- Supremo commodity: Base pricing
- Regional specialty: Moderate premium
- Single-farm micro-lots: Premium pricing
- Competition winners: High premium

## Brewing Recommendations

### For Washed Colombian

**Best Methods:** V60, Chemex, Kalita Wave

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Roast | Light to medium |
| Grind | Medium-fine |
| Ratio | 1:16-1:17 |
| Temp | 93-96°C |
| Time | 2:30-3:30 |

### As Espresso

**Versatile for all espresso applications.**

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Dose | 18-20g |
| Yield | 36-45g |
| Time | 28-35s |
| Notes | Works well in milk drinks |

### For Cold Brew
- Medium roast preferred
- 1:8-1:12 ratio
- 12-18 hours steep
- Clean, sweet, chocolatey result

## Colombian Coffee Culture

### Federation Nacional de Cafeteros (FNC)
- Represents 500,000+ coffee families
- Quality control and export regulation
- Research center (Cenicafé)
- Juan Valdez brand

### Smallholder Production
- Average farm: 1.5-2 hectares
- Family-owned and operated
- Manual selective harvesting
- Community washing stations common

### Direct Trade
- Growing specialty market
- Roasters visiting farms common
- Transparent pricing initiatives
- Cup of Excellence participation
