# Brazil Coffee Guide

Brazil is the world's largest coffee producer by a significant margin, accounting for roughly one-third of global production.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Ranking** | #1 largest producer globally |
| **Production** | ~60-65 million bags annually |
| **Specialty %** | ~15-20% specialty grade |
| **Main Species** | Arabica (~70%), Robusta (~30%) |
| **Varieties** | Bourbon, Catuai, Mundo Novo, Yellow Bourbon |
| **Harvest** | May - September |
| **Altitude** | 800 - 1,400+ masl |

## Why Brazil Matters

### Scale & Influence
- Produces more than #2, #3, and #4 combined
- Sets global commodity pricing
- Year-round supply stability
- Major influence on market trends

### Unique Characteristics
- Mechanized harvesting (flat terrain)
- Natural processing dominant
- Lower altitude than most origins
- Distinctive chocolate/nut profile

## Brazilian Varieties

### Yellow Bourbon
- **Background**: Bourbon mutation, yellow cherry
- **Cup Profile**: Sweet, complex, fruity acidity
- **Notes**: Often commands premium, excellent specialty potential

### Red Bourbon
- **Background**: Traditional Bourbon
- **Cup Profile**: Sweet, balanced, complex
- **Notes**: Less common than Yellow

### Catuai
- **Background**: Mundo Novo x Caturra cross
- **Cup Profile**: Sweet, balanced, mild
- **Prevalence**: Very common
- **Notes**: Good productivity, adaptable

### Mundo Novo
- **Background**: Bourbon x Typica hybrid
- **Cup Profile**: Balanced, sweet, mild
- **Prevalence**: Common
- **Notes**: High yield, disease resistant

### Acaia
- **Background**: Mundo Novo selection
- **Cup Profile**: Clean, mild
- **Notes**: Large beans

### Obatã
- **Background**: Rust-resistant variety
- **Cup Profile**: Lower cup quality
- **Notes**: Production variety

### Topázio
- **Background**: Yellow cherry variety
- **Cup Profile**: Sweet, mild
- **Notes**: Good specialty potential

## Processing in Brazil

### Natural (Dry) Process
- **Dominance**: ~70% of production
- **Why**: Dry climate, large scale, tradition
- **Result**: Heavy body, sweet, fruit notes
- **Risk**: Ferment if poorly managed

### Pulped Natural (Honey)
- **Growing popularity**: ~25%
- **Process**: Depulp, dry with mucilage
- **Result**: Cleaner than natural, good sweetness
- **Common in**: Specialty production

### Washed
- **Less common**: ~5%
- **Why rare**: Water scarcity, tradition
- **Result**: Cleaner, brighter
- **Found in**: Specialty experimental lots

### Experimental Processing
- Anaerobic fermentation growing
- Extended fermentation
- Temperature-controlled processing
- Competition lots

## Brazilian Grading System

### Screen Size

| Grade | Screen Size |
|-------|-------------|
| Screen 19 | Large |
| Screen 17/18 | Good |
| Screen 15/16 | Standard |
| Screen 14 | Small |

### Defect-Based Grading

| Grade | Max Defects | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Strictly Soft (SS) | Very low | Top specialty |
| Soft | Low | Good specialty |
| Softish | Moderate | Commercial specialty |
| Hard | Higher | Commercial |
| Rioy | Many | Low quality |

### Cup Quality Terms
- **Strictly Soft**: Clean, sweet, no defects
- **Soft**: Minor defects, good cup
- **Hard**: Some harshness
- **Rioy**: Iodine/medicinal off-flavor

## Brazil for Espresso

### Why Brazil Excels for Espresso
- Reliable, consistent base
- Low acidity = smooth shots
- Chocolate, nut notes
- Full body, crema production
- Cost-effective

## Buying Brazilian Coffee

### What to Look For
- Region specified (Sul de Minas, Cerrado, etc.)
- Processing method
- Screen size/grade
- Variety (Yellow Bourbon = premium)
- Farm or cooperative name
- Cup score (specialty: 80+)

### Quality Indicators
- Single variety lots
- Altitude noted (higher = better)
- Specific farm traceable
- Recent harvest date

### Price Expectations
- Commodity: Base pricing
- Good specialty: Moderate
- Yellow Bourbon/competition: Premium
- Cup of Excellence winners: High premium

## Brewing Recommendations

### For Natural Brazilian

**Best Methods:** Espresso, French Press, Cold Brew

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Roast | Medium to medium-dark |
| Grind | Method-appropriate |
| Ratio | 1:15-1:16 (immersion), 1:2 (espresso) |
| Temp | 90-93°C |
| Notes | Forgiving, versatile |

### For Pulped Natural

**Best Methods:** All methods work well

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Roast | Light-medium to medium |
| Grind | Slightly finer |
| Ratio | 1:16-1:17 |
| Temp | 92-94°C |
| Notes | Balanced approach |

### Cold Brew with Brazilian
- Excellent base for cold brew
- Smooth, chocolate-forward
- Low acidity amplified
- 1:8 to 1:12 ratio
- 16-20 hours steep
