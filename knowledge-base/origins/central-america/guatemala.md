# Guatemala Coffee Guide

Guatemala produces distinctive, high-quality Arabica coffee across eight designated growing regions, each with unique terroir and flavor profiles.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Ranking** | ~10th largest producer globally |
| **Production** | ~3.5-4 million bags annually |
| **Specialty %** | ~40% specialty grade |
| **Main Species** | 100% Arabica |
| **Varieties** | Bourbon, Caturra, Catuai, Typica, Pache |
| **Harvest** | December - April |
| **Altitude** | 1,300 - 2,000+ masl |

## Why Guatemala is Special

### Volcanic Soil
- 37 volcanoes, many active
- Rich mineral content
- Exceptional terroir
- Distinctive cup character

### Eight Distinct Regions
- Anacafé designates growing regions
- Each has unique microclimate
- Regional identity in cups
- Traceability standard

### Quality Heritage
- Long specialty coffee history
- Strong direct trade relationships
- Cup of Excellence participant
- Well-organized sector

## The Eight Growing Regions

### Antigua

**Guatemala's most famous region.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,500-1,700 masl |
| Processing | Washed |
| Harvest | January-March |
| Soil | Volcanic, pumice-rich |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Full body
- Rich, complex
- Chocolate, spice
- Smoky undertones
- Balanced acidity

**Notable Features:**
- Valley between three volcanoes
- Protected denomination of origin
- Premium pricing
- Highly sought after

### Huehuetenango

**Highest altitude, distinctive profile.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,500-2,000+ masl |
| Processing | Washed |
| Harvest | January-April |
| Climate | Dry, hot winds from Mexico |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Wine-like acidity
- Stone fruit
- Floral notes
- Complex, layered
- Medium body

**Notable Features:**
- Non-volcanic (limestone)
- Remote, difficult access
- Exceptional quality lots
- Competition winners

### Cobán

**Rainforest microclimate.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,300-1,500 masl |
| Processing | Washed |
| Harvest | December-March |
| Climate | Cool, rainy, misty |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Bright acidity
- Fruity (berries)
- Light body
- Distinctive character
- Floral undertones

**Notable Features:**
- Cloud forest region
- Unique "chipi-chipi" mist
- Q'eqchi' Maya producers

### Atitlán

**Lake Atitlán volcanic region.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,500-1,700 masl |
| Processing | Washed |
| Harvest | December-March |
| Soil | Very rich volcanic |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Citrus acidity
- Full body
- Nutty, chocolate
- Well-balanced
- Clean finish

**Notable Features:**
- Stunning beauty

### San Marcos

**Warmest and wettest region.**

- Light body

## Guatemalan Varieties

### Bourbon
- **Cup Profile**: Sweet, complex, fruity
- **Prevalence**: Common in highlands
- **Notes**: Traditional, excellent quality

### Caturra
- **Cup Profile**: Bright, citrus, light body
- **Prevalence**: Very common
- **Notes**: Productive, quality-focused

### Catuai
- **Cup Profile**: Clean, balanced
- **Prevalence**: Common
- **Notes**: Bourbon x Caturra cross

### Typica
- **Cup Profile**: Sweet, clean, complex
- **Prevalence**: Less common
- **Notes**: Traditional variety

### Pache
- **Cup Profile**: Smooth, balanced
- **Prevalence**: Common in Antigua
- **Notes**: Typica mutation, Guatemalan origin

### Pacamara
- **Cup Profile**: Complex, fruity, floral
- **Prevalence**: Rare, competition lots
- **Notes**: Large bean, exceptional cups

### Gesha
- **Cup Profile**: Jasmine, bergamot, tea-like
- **Prevalence**: Very limited
- **Notes**: Premium pricing

## Processing in Guatemala

### Washed (Fully Washed)
- **Dominance**: ~95% of production
- **Method**: Traditional fermentation
- **Duration**: 12-36 hours typical
- **Result**: Clean, bright, terroir-focused

### Honey Process
- **Growing**: ~3-5% and increasing
- **Method**: Mucilage retained during drying
- **Result**: Added sweetness, body
- **Found in**: Specialty lots

### Natural Process
- **Limited**: ~1-2%
- **Method**: Whole cherry dried
- **Result**: Fruity, heavy body
- **Found in**: Experimental lots

## Guatemalan Grading

### Altitude-Based Grades

| Grade | Altitude | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| SHB (Strictly Hard Bean) | 1,370+ masl | Highest quality |
| HB (Hard Bean) | 1,220-1,370 masl | Good quality |
| Semi-Hard | 1,050-1,220 masl | Standard |
| EPW (Extra Prime Washed) | 900-1,050 masl | Commercial |
| Prime Washed | Below 900 masl | Lower grade |

### Quality Terms
- **Strictly Hard Bean (SHB)**: Specialty standard
- **Fancy**: High-quality SHB
- **EP (European Preparation)**: Low defects

## Buying Guatemalan Coffee

### What to Look For
- Region specified (Antigua, Huehue, etc.)
- Farm or cooperative name
- SHB grade
- Variety information
- Processing method
- Altitude noted
- Harvest year

### Quality Indicators
- Single farm traceable
- High altitude (1,500+ masl)
- Specific variety
- Cup score available

### Price Expectations
- Antigua: Premium pricing
- Huehuetenango: Premium for quality
- Other regions: Moderate specialty
- Competition lots: High premium

## Brewing Recommendations

### For Washed Guatemalan

**Best Methods:** Pour over, drip, espresso

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Roast | Light to medium |
| Grind | Medium-fine |
| Ratio | 1:16-1:17 |
| Temp | 93-96°C |
| Time | 2:30-3:30 (pour over) |

### For Espresso

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Dose | 18-20g |
| Yield | 36-42g |
| Time | 26-32s |
| Notes | Excellent in milk drinks |

### Regional Brewing Tips
- **Antigua**: Medium roast, espresso-friendly
- **Huehuetenango**: Light roast, pour over
- **Cobán**: Light roast, highlight acidity
