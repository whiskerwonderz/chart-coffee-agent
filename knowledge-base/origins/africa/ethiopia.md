# Ethiopia Coffee Guide

Ethiopia is the birthplace of Arabica coffee and produces some of the world's most distinctive and sought-after specialty coffees.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Ranking** | 5th largest producer globally |
| **Production** | ~7.5 million bags annually |
| **Specialty %** | ~55% graded as specialty |
| **Main Species** | Arabica (indigenous) |
| **Varieties** | 5,000+ indigenous varieties |
| **Harvest** | October - February |
| **Altitude** | 1,400 - 2,200+ masl |

## Why Ethiopia is Special

### Genetic Diversity
- Birthplace of Coffea Arabica
- Over 5,000 indigenous varieties
- Most genetically diverse coffee origin
- Many varieties unnamed ("heirloom")

### Unique Flavor Profiles
- Intensely aromatic
- Complex, often tea-like
- Fruit-forward (washed and natural)
- Floral notes common
- Wine-like characteristics in naturals

## Major Growing Regions

### Yirgacheffe

**The most famous Ethiopian region.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,700-2,200 masl |
| Processing | Washed and Natural |
| Harvest | October-January |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Floral (jasmine, bergamot)
- Citrus (lemon, lime)
- Tea-like body
- Bright acidity
- Clean, delicate

**Sub-regions:**
- Gedeb
- Kochere
- Aricha
- Idido

### Sidama (Sidamo)

**Large, diverse region south of Yirgacheffe.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,500-2,200 masl |
| Processing | Washed and Natural |
| Harvest | October-January |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Citrus and stone fruit
- Berry notes (naturals)
- Floral undertones
- Medium body
- Wine-like (naturals)

**Notable Areas:**
- Bensa
- Dale
- Chire
- Nensebo

### Guji

**Newer region, split from Sidama in 2010.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,800-2,300 masl |
| Processing | Washed and Natural |
| Harvest | October-February |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Intense fruit (natural process)
- Blueberry, strawberry
- Complex, layered
- Heavy body (naturals)
- Wine-like, boozy

**Notable Areas:**
- Hambela
- Shakiso
- Uraga

### Harrar (Harar)

**Eastern region, ancient coffee tradition.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,500-2,100 masl |
| Processing | Natural (traditionally) |
| Harvest | October-February |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Wild, fruity
- Blueberry, wine
- Earthy, spicy
- Heavy body
- Fermented notes

**Characteristics:**
- Dry-processed tradition
- Small farms, wild coffee
- Distinctive "mocha" character

### Limu

**Western region, lesser known but quality.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,400-2,100 masl |
| Processing | Mostly washed |
| Harvest | October-January |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Wine-like, spicy
- Low acidity
- Medium body
- Balanced sweetness

### Djimmah (Jimma)

**High-volume, commercial region.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Altitude | 1,400-2,000 masl |
| Processing | Washed and Natural |
| Harvest | October-January |

**Flavor Profile:**
- Earthy, less complex
- Lower acidity
- Used in blends
- Occasional specialty lots

## Processing in Ethiopia

### Washed (Wet) Process
- Dominates Yirgacheffe and Sidama
- Clean, bright cups
- Floral and citrus notes prominent
- 12-36 hour fermentation typical

### Natural (Dry) Process
- Traditional method
- Intense fruit character
- Heavy body
- Blueberry, wine notes
- Can be inconsistent if poorly managed

### Honey Process
- Emerging method
- Combines qualities of both
- Growing in popularity

## Grading System

### Ethiopian Coffee Grades

| Grade | Defects | Quality |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Grade 1 | 0-3 | Top specialty |
| Grade 2 | 4-12 | Specialty |
| Grade 3 | 13-25 | Commercial specialty |
| Grade 4 | 26-45 | Commercial |
| Grade 5 | 46-100 | Low commercial |

**Note:** Grades apply to both washed and natural separately.

### Quality Descriptors
- **Specialty**: Grade 1-2
- **Commercial**: Grade 3-5

## Coffee Culture in Ethiopia

### Importance
- Coffee accounts for ~30% of export earnings
- 15+ million people depend on coffee
- Deep cultural significance

### Traditional Ceremony
- Coffee ceremony is central to social life
- Beans roasted fresh
- Three rounds: Abol, Tona, Baraka
- Takes 2-3 hours
- Sign of respect and friendship

### Consumption
- Ethiopians consume 50% of production domestically
- Highest per-capita consumption in Africa
- "Buna dabo naw" - "Coffee is our bread"

## Buying Ethiopian Coffee

### What to Look For
- Region and washing station specified
- Processing method clearly stated
- Grade indicated (G1 or G2 for specialty)
- Altitude information
- Harvest year

### Seasonality
- Fresh crop arrives: January-April
- Peak availability: March-July
- Plan purchases around harvest

### Price Expectations
- G1 Yirgacheffe: Premium pricing
- G2 Sidama: Moderate specialty pricing
- Natural Guji: Premium for exceptional lots

## Brewing Recommendations

### For Washed Ethiopian

**Best Methods:** V60, Chemex, AeroPress

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Roast | Light to light-medium |
| Grind | Medium-fine |
| Ratio | 1:16-1:17 |
| Temp | 94-96°C |
| Time | 2:30-3:30 |

### For Natural Ethiopian

**Best Methods:** Any pour over, French Press

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Roast | Light to medium |
| Grind | Medium |
| Ratio | 1:15-1:16 |
| Temp | 92-94°C |
| Time | 3:00-4:00 |

### As Espresso
- Light roast: Bright, fruity shots
- Increase ratio (1:2.5) for clarity
- Lower temp for naturals
- Excellent for milk drinks (natural process)
