# Espresso Fundamentals

Espresso is a concentrated coffee brewing method that forces hot water through finely ground coffee under pressure, producing a rich, intense beverage with characteristic crema.

## What Defines Espresso?

### Key Characteristics
- **Pressure**: 9 bars (130 PSI) standard
- **Time**: 25-35 seconds extraction
- **Temperature**: 90-96°C (194-205°F)
- **Concentration**: 8-12% TDS
- **Volume**: 25-50ml typical

### Espresso vs Other Methods
| Factor | Espresso | Filter Coffee |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Pressure | 9 bars | Gravity only |
| Grind | Very fine | Medium |
| Time | 25-35 sec | 2-5 min |
| TDS | 8-12% | 1.2-1.5% |
| Body | Heavy, syrupy | Light to medium |
| Crema | Yes | No |

## Essential Equipment

### The Espresso Machine
**Key Components:**
- Boiler (single, dual, or heat exchanger)
- Pump (vibratory or rotary)
- Grouphead
- Portafilter
- Steam wand
- PID temperature controller (modern machines)

**Machine Types:**
| Type | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| Single Boiler | Affordable | Wait between brewing/steaming |
| Heat Exchanger | Simultaneous brew/steam | Temperature less precise |
| Dual Boiler | Best control | Expensive |

### The Grinder
**Critical features:**
- Burr type (flat vs conical)
- Stepless adjustment preferred
- Low retention
- Consistent particle distribution

**Recommended Grinders:**
- Entry: Baratza Sette, 1Zpresso J-Max
- Mid: Eureka Mignon, DF64
- High-end: Niche Zero, Eureka Atom
- Commercial: Mazzer, Mahlkönig EK43

### Accessories
| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Tamper | Compress coffee puck |
| Distribution tool (WDT) | Even distribution |
| Distributor/leveler | Consistent tamping |
| Scale | Measure dose and yield |
| Timer | Track extraction time |
| Knock box | Dispose of pucks |

## Espresso Recipes

### Traditional Espresso Ratios

| Style | Ratio | Dose | Yield | Time |
|-------|-------|------|-------|------|
| Ristretto | 1:1 - 1:1.5 | 18g | 18-27g | 20-25s |
| Normale | 1:2 - 1:2.5 | 18g | 36-45g | 25-30s |
| Lungo | 1:3 - 1:4 | 18g | 54-72g | 35-45s |

### Modern Approaches

**Turbo Shot:**
- Ratio: 1:2.5-1:3
- Coarser grind, higher flow
- Time: 15-20 seconds
- Result: Bright, clean, less bitter

**Allongé:**
- Ratio: 1:4-1:5
- Extended extraction
- Higher clarity
- American-style espresso

## The Espresso Process

### Step 1: Dose
1. Weigh coffee dose (typically 18-20g for double)
2. Consistency is key
3. Use single-dose or weigh each time

**Dosing Guidelines:**
| Basket Size | Dose Range |
|-------------|------------|
| Single (7-12g) | 7-10g |
| Double (14-21g) | 16-20g |
| Triple (21-24g) | 20-22g |

### Step 2: Distribute
1. Break up clumps
2. Use WDT (Weiss Distribution Technique)
3. Create even bed
4. Optional: Use distribution tool

**WDT Technique:**
- Use fine needle or WDT tool
- Stir grounds in circular motion
- Breaks clumps, improves uniformity
- Reduces channeling

### Step 3: Tamp
1. Level the grounds first
2. Apply 15-30 lbs pressure
3. Keep tamper level
4. Twist optional (polish)
5. Consistent pressure matters more than amount

### Step 4: Extract
1. Lock portafilter immediately
2. Start shot, start timer
3. Watch for channeling
4. Target time and yield
5. Stop at target weight

### Step 5: Evaluate
1. Visual: Crema color, tiger striping
2. Taste: Balance, sweetness, acidity
3. Texture: Body, mouthfeel
4. Adjust variables as needed

## Dialing In

### The Process
1. Start with baseline recipe (1:2, 18g:36g, 30s)
2. Pull shot, taste
3. Identify issue (sour/bitter)
4. Adjust ONE variable
5. Repeat until balanced

### Adjustment Guide

**Shot runs too fast (<20s):**
- Grind finer
- Or increase dose slightly

**Shot runs too slow (>35s):**
- Grind coarser
- Or decrease dose slightly

**Taste Adjustments:**

| Taste | Problem | Solution |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Sour, sharp | Under-extracted | Grind finer, longer time |
| Bitter, harsh | Over-extracted | Grind coarser, shorter time |
| Thin, watery | Low extraction | Finer grind, higher temp |
| Astringent | Over-extracted | Coarser grind, lower yield |
| Flat, dull | Stale coffee | Use fresher beans |

## Troubleshooting

### Channeling
**Signs:** Uneven extraction, spurting, blonde spots
**Causes:**
- Poor distribution
- Uneven tamp
- Cracked puck
- Worn gaskets

**Solutions:**
- Use WDT tool
- Level before tamping
- Check basket for damage
- Replace gaskets

### No Crema
**Causes:**
- Stale coffee (>4 weeks)
- Oily beans
- Grind too coarse
- Temperature too low

**Solutions:**
- Use fresher coffee
- Clean equipment
- Adjust grind finer
- Check machine temperature

### Sour Shots Consistently
**Systematic Causes:**
- Machine temperature too low
- Grinder needs calibration
- Water too soft
- Coffee too fresh (needs degassing)

### Bitter Shots Consistently
**Systematic Causes:**
- Machine temperature too high
- Grinder producing excess fines
- Water too hard
- Dark roast coffee

## Water Temperature

### Temperature Guidelines
| Roast Level | Temperature |
|-------------|-------------|
| Light | 94-96°C (201-205°F) |
| Medium | 92-94°C (198-201°F) |
| Dark | 90-92°C (194-198°F) |

### Temperature Surfing (Single Boiler)
1. Flush until boiler activates
2. Wait for heating element to turn off
3. Brew immediately
4. Consistent timing is key

## Crema

### What is Crema?
- Emulsion of CO2, oils, and solids
- Produced by pressure extraction
- Indicates freshness
- Contains bitter compounds

### Ideal Crema Characteristics
- Thick, persistent (2-3mm)
- Golden-brown color
- Tiger striping pattern
- Lasts 2+ minutes

### Crema Issues
| Issue | Cause |
|-------|-------|
| No crema | Stale beans, grind too coarse |
| White/blonde | Under-extraction |
| Dark/thin | Over-extraction |
| Dissipates quickly | Beans past prime |

## Milk-Based Drinks

### Standard Recipes

| Drink | Espresso | Milk | Foam |
|-------|----------|------|------|
| Macchiato | Single | Dollop | Minimal |
| Cortado | Double | Equal | Minimal |
| Flat White | Double | 5-6oz | Microfoam |
| Cappuccino | Single/Double | 5-6oz | Thick foam |
| Latte | Double | 8-12oz | Thin foam |

### Milk Steaming
1. Purge steam wand
2. Submerge tip just below surface
3. Stretch (add air) first 3-5 seconds
4. Submerge and spin to texture
5. Target 55-65°C (131-149°F)
6. Tap and swirl to integrate

## Maintenance

### Daily
- Purge grouphead between shots
- Wipe steam wand after each use
- Backflush with water (end of day)
- Empty drip tray and knock box

### Weekly
- Backflush with detergent
- Clean steam wand thoroughly
- Wipe exterior
- Check gaskets

### Monthly
- Deep clean grouphead
- Descale if needed
- Check grinder burrs
- Lubricate moving parts
