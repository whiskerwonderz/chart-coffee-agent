# Hario V60 Brewing Guide

The Hario V60 is one of the most popular pour-over brewing devices, known for producing clean, bright cups that highlight a coffee's unique characteristics.

## About the V60

- **Invented**: 2004 by Hario (Japan)
- **Name Origin**: "V" shape at 60-degree angle
- **Material Options**: Ceramic, glass, plastic, metal, copper
- **Sizes**: 01 (1-2 cups), 02 (1-4 cups), 03 (1-6 cups)

### Design Features
- **60° angle cone**: Optimizes extraction flow
- **Single large hole**: Allows control over flow rate
- **Spiral ribs**: Create air channels, prevent filter sticking
- **Thin paper filters**: Produce clean cup, absorb oils

## Basic Recipe

### Standard Parameters
- **Coffee**: 15-20g
- **Water**: 250-340g
- **Ratio**: 1:15 to 1:17 (60-65g per liter)
- **Grind**: Medium-fine (like table salt)
- **Temperature**: 92-96°C (197-205°F)
- **Total time**: 2:30-3:30

### Scaling Guide

| Coffee | Water | Cups |
|--------|-------|------|
| 15g | 250g | 1 |
| 20g | 340g | 1-2 |
| 30g | 500g | 2-3 |
| 45g | 750g | 3-4 |

## Step-by-Step Method

### Preparation
1. Boil water (heat extra for rinsing)
2. Fold filter along seam, place in V60
3. Rinse filter with hot water thoroughly
4. Discard rinse water
5. Grind coffee medium-fine
6. Add grounds to filter, shake to level
7. Create small well in center (optional)
8. Place on scale, tare to zero

### Brewing Process

#### Phase 1: Bloom (0:00-0:45)
1. Start timer
2. Pour 2-3x coffee weight in water (30-60g)
3. Ensure all grounds are saturated
4. Swirl gently or stir with spoon
5. Wait 30-45 seconds for degassing

#### Phase 2: Main Pour (0:45-2:00)
**Single Pour Method:**
1. Pour in slow, steady spirals
2. Start from center, move outward
3. Avoid pouring on filter paper
4. Keep water level consistent
5. Finish pour by 1:30-2:00

**Pulse Pour Method:**
1. Pour in 3-4 pulses of 50-100g each
2. Wait for water to drain between pulses
3. Maintain consistent intervals
4. Finish total water by 2:00

#### Phase 3: Drawdown (2:00-3:30)
1. Allow water to fully drain
2. Give dripper gentle swirl at end
3. Target flat coffee bed
4. Total time: 2:30-3:30

### Post-Brew
1. Remove dripper immediately
2. Swirl server to mix
3. Serve or transfer to thermal carafe
4. Note grind adjustments for next time

## James Hoffmann's Ultimate V60 Technique

A refined method developed by World Barista Champion James Hoffmann:

### Parameters
- Coffee: 30g
- Water: 500g
- Ratio: 1:16.7
- Temp: 93-95°C
- Grind: Medium-fine

### Process
1. **Rinse**: Thoroughly rinse filter
2. **Add coffee**: Create well in center
3. **Bloom (0:00)**: Pour 60g (2x dose), swirl
4. **Wait**: 45 seconds
5. **First pour (0:45)**: Pour to 300g by 1:15
6. **Second pour (1:15)**: Pour to 500g by 1:45
7. **Stir**: Gentle stir clockwise, then counter-clockwise
8. **Swirl**: Single swirl to settle grounds
9. **Drain**: Should finish 3:00-3:30

### Key Points
- Swirling bloom ensures even saturation
- Two-stage pour maintains temperature
- Final stir dislodges grounds from sides
- Flat bed indicates even extraction

## Troubleshooting

### Coffee Tastes Sour (Under-extracted)
| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Grind too coarse | Grind finer |
| Water too cool | Increase temperature |
| Pour too fast | Slow down pour |
| Ratio too high | Use more coffee |

### Coffee Tastes Bitter (Over-extracted)
| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Grind too fine | Grind coarser |
| Water too hot | Decrease temperature |
| Brew time too long | Pour faster |
| Ratio too low | Use less coffee |

### Uneven Extraction
| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Channeling | Improve pour technique |
| Uneven bed | Level grounds before brewing |
| Inconsistent pour | Practice spiral motion |
| Bypass (high/dry sides) | Pour closer to center |

### Drawdown Issues
| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Too fast (<2:00) | Grind too coarse | Grind finer |
| Too slow (>4:00) | Grind too fine | Grind coarser |
| Stalling | Fines clogging | Coarser grind, less agitation |

## Advanced Techniques

### Pre-wetting (Rao Spin)
1. After bloom, give gentle swirl
2. Creates even slurry
3. Improves extraction uniformity

### Bypass Brewing
1. Use less water through coffee
2. Add hot water to server
3. Reduces extraction percentage
4. Useful for light roasts

### Ice Flash Brew (Japanese Iced Coffee)
1. Place ice in server (40% of total water)
2. Brew with remaining 60% hot water
3. Stronger ratio: 1:12-1:14
4. Coffee brews directly onto ice

## Tips for Success

1. **Use fresh coffee**: 7-21 days post-roast ideal
2. **Pre-heat everything**: Dripper, server, cup
3. **Consistent pouring**: Practice makes perfect
4. **Take notes**: Record variables and results
5. **Taste and adjust**: One variable at a time
6. **Water quality matters**: Use filtered water
